SMS Consent & Opt-In

Last updated: July 2026
This page documents how callers consent to receive a single post-call confirmation text message from a FlatRateVoice AI voice agent. This is a verbal, on-call opt-in — no website form is involved in this consent flow.

Who this applies to

When a caller phones a business using a FlatRateVoice AI voice agent, the agent answers the call, has a natural conversation, and — before the call ends — verbally asks the caller for permission to send a single text message confirming what was discussed (for example, an appointment time). The caller's spoken response is the opt-in.

The consent script

This is a verbal opt-in collected live on the phone call — there is no web form or checkbox involved, because the caller never visits a website. The AI agent asks the question below out loud and listens for the caller's spoken answer.

Every call includes this exchange, spoken by the AI agent, before the call ends:

AI Agent"Before we wrap up — is it okay if we text you a quick confirmation of what we discussed today, like your appointment time? We'll send one message per call, message and data rates may apply, reply HELP for help, reply STOP to opt out."
Caller"Yes, that works."
AI Agent"Great, you'll get that text in just a moment."

This consent flow is governed by our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

What happens with the caller's answer

  • If the caller agrees, the system sends exactly one SMS confirming the details discussed on the call.
  • If the caller declines, no text message is sent.
  • Message frequency: one message per call, only when consent is given.
  • Message and data rates may apply.
  • Reply STOP at any time to opt out, or HELP for help.

Sample confirmation message

"Thanks for calling [Business Name]! We've got your info and will follow up shortly. Call us back at [Business Phone]. Reply STOP to unsubscribe."

How consent is recorded

The AI agent listens for the caller's spoken answer to the consent question above. That answer is captured as a structured yes/no signal in the call system in real time — not inferred or guessed. The confirmation text is sent only when that signal is affirmative. If the caller says no, declines, or doesn't give a clear yes, no text is sent and the call still completes normally.

Data use

Phone numbers collected through this flow are used only to send the single opt-in confirmation text described above. We do not sell this information. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
